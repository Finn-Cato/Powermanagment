Device priority

Toggle a device on to let Power Guard control it. Use the ↑↓ arrows to set the order — devices higher in the list are turned off first.

How does priority order work?
Priority list order — how to prioritise EV charging:
Devices at the top of the priority list are shed first when power exceeds the limit. Devices at the bottom are shed last. To prioritise your car charging, move EV chargers to the bottom of the list — this way heaters and water heaters are reduced before the charger is touched.

Example priority list (chargers prioritised):
1🔥 Water heater← shed first
2🌡️ Floor heating — Bathroom
3🌡️ Floor heating — Hallway
4🌡️ Panel heater — Living room
5🌡️ Panel heater — Bedroom
6🔌 EV Charger← shed last
If you want heaters to stay on and the charger to be reduced first, move the charger to the top instead.

Getting Started

  1. Go to ⚙️ Settings and set your Maximum power (W) — this is your grid connection limit (e.g. 10 000 W).
  2. Go to the 📱 Devices tab and toggle on the devices you want Power Guard to control. Use the ↑↓ arrows to set priority order — devices at the top are turned off first.
  3. Turn on Power Guard in ⚙️ Settings.

Power Guard (Core)

Power Guard reads your HAN electricity meter in real time. When your total consumption approaches the grid limit, it turns off or reduces devices — starting from the top of the priority list. When power drops back down, devices are automatically restored.

  • Normal profile — acts at 100% of your limit.
  • Strict profile — acts at 95% of your limit for extra headroom.
  • Phase limits — set per-phase ampere limits (L1/L2/L3) in Advanced settings.
  • Smart restore — devices are only turned back on when there is confirmed headroom; charger ramping power is accounted for before restoring heating devices.
Ignore power reading (Ignorer effektmåling):
Some devices (e.g. smart plugs with broken power metering) report 0 W even when they are actively drawing power. Power Guard normally skips such devices to avoid wasting a mitigation slot on something that is idle. If you know a device is actually consuming power but reports 0 W, tick Ignore power reading on that device in the 📱 Devices tab. Power Guard will then control it regardless of what the power sensor reports.

Norsk: Noen enheter (f.eks. smarte stikkontakter med ødelagt effektmåling) rapporterer 0 W selv om de faktisk trekker strøm. Huk av Ignorer effektmåling for enheten i 📱 Enheter-fanen, så styres den av Power Guard uansett hva strømmåleren viser.

Smart EV Charging

Enable Smart Price Control in Settings to activate smart EV charging. All chargers share the same cheapest-hours window — the car that needs the most time sets the window size, and available power is split equally between chargers during those hours. Set up each charger in 4 steps:

  1. Devices tab — enable your EV charger and set action to Dynamic Current.
  2. Devices tab — fill in Battery size (kWh), Charge to (%), and link a Car device under the charger row.
  3. 📊 System tab — set the circuit breaker limit (A) for the charger.
  4. 🧠 Smart → EV tab — set Ready by (deadline). Hours needed is calculated automatically per charger from the linked car's battery.
How hours are calculated (per charger):
kWh needed = (target% − current%) ÷ 100 × battery capacity
Charger power = circuit limit (A) × 230 V × phases
Hours needed = kWh needed ÷ charger power (kW)

Example: 40% → 80%, 77 kWh battery, 16A 1-phase = (80−40)/100 × 77 ÷ 3.7 kW ≈ 8.3 hours

When a deadline is set, Power Guard picks the N cheapest hours before the deadline (N = whichever car needs the most hours) and charges all chargers during those hours. All other hours all chargers are Off. If the deadline is imminent, it forces full charging regardless of price. With two chargers, the available current is split equally so both cars charge at the same time.

Current stepping:
Power Guard uses simple 1A steps to control charger current. It steps down immediately on every HAN reading when consumption is over the limit. It steps up a maximum of 1A every 60 seconds — giving the meter time to confirm each increase before the next. On restart, the charger always resumes from 6A so high-current chargers are stepped down right away if needed.

Charging complete detection:
When a car finishes charging (session completed, power drops below 100W) but stays plugged in, Power Guard recognises the session as done — no mismatch alerts are raised and heating devices are not shed on behalf of a charger that is no longer actively charging.
EV charger buffer:
Found in Settings, just below Protection mode. Set a watt value (e.g. 500 W or 1000 W) that Power Guard reserves for the rest of the house before allowing the charger to ramp up. The charger will only increase current if the available headroom exceeds the normal step threshold plus this buffer. Step-down is unaffected — the charger still reduces immediately when the house goes over the limit. Set to 0 to disable.
What the Charger Mode label means:
⚡ Fast charge cheap hour — this is one of the cheapest hours, charging at full speed
⏸ Off waiting for cheap hour — not a cheap hour, charger is paused to save money
🐢 Slow charge no deadline set — no deadline configured, charging at reduced speed regardless of price
▶ Charging — no deadline set, price is normal, charging without restriction
Charger priority shed:
Each charger in the 📱 Devices tab has a Priority shed toggle. When enabled, the charger is treated like any other device in the priority list — Power Guard can pause it to protect the grid limit. On/off type chargers are shed even when they are reporting 0W (e.g. idle between steps).
Flow-controlled charger:
Tick this for chargers that Power Guard cannot control directly (no native Homey driver). When enabled, Power Guard fires Homey flow trigger cards instead of calling the charger API — you build flows to handle the commands.
Automatisk fasedeteksjon:
Power Guard detekterer automatisk om laderen er 1-fase eller 3-fase — fungerer for både TN-nett (400V) og IT-nett (230V L-L).
Lad nå (Kriseknapp):
Den virtuelle enheten Lad nå overstyrer prisbasert pause — laderen får kjøre fritt opp til watt-grensen, uavhengig av spotpris. Nyttig når du trenger å lade umiddelbart uansett pris.

Når aktiv vises en oransje kant på elbil-kortet i Hjem-fanen med teksten Ignorerer pris - kriseknappen aktivert.

Når Lad nå slås av, settes laderen umiddelbart på pause og normal ramp-logikk tar over — prismotoren bestemmer om og når ladingen skal fortsette fra 6A.
EV Batteri (manuell batteriprosent):
Har du ikke en bil-app som rapporterer batterinivå automatisk? Legg til en EV Batteri-enhet fra Power Guard i Homey (Legg til enhet → Power Guard → EV Batteri), velg hvilken lader den tilhører, og koble den til laderen i BIL-feltet på laderkortet.

Deretter kan du ganske enkelt dra glidebryteren på EV Batteri-enheten til nåværende batteriprosent. Power Guard bruker verdien til å beregne hvor mange timer det tar å lade ferdig og planlegger ladevinduet deretter.
To biler, én lader:
Hvis du deler én lader mellom to biler, bruk flow-aksjonen Angi aktiv bil for å fortelle Power Guard hvilken bil som er tilkoblet.

Opprett en flyt: NÅR bil kobles til → SÅ Power Guard: Angi aktiv bil → velg lader, oppgi batteristørrelse (kWh) og lademål (%). Power Guard bruker disse verdiene de neste 48 timene, og faller deretter tilbake til laderens konfigurerte innstillinger.
Homey-tidslinjevarsler:
Power Guard sender automatisk varsler til Homey-tidslinjen din for disse hendelsene:
✅ Ferdig ladet — bilen er full og ladesyklusen er avsluttet
⏸ Pauset — dyr strøm — prisstyringen har satt ladingen på pause
▶ Lading gjenopptatt — billig time — ladingen er startet opp igjen etter en prispause

Ingen flow-oppsett nødvendig — varsler sendes automatisk.

Modes (Home / Night / Away / Holiday)

Find modes under 🧠 Smart → Modes (when Smart Price Control is ON) or 🌡️ Heaters tab (when OFF). Each mode lets you set per-device preferences:

  • Switches — On / Off
  • Thermostats — target temperature
  • EV chargers — Allow / Pause
  • Høiax water heaters — High / Medium / Low / Off
What the buttons do:

— → Do nothing. The device is left completely alone when the mode activates.
Off → Turn the device off when the mode activates. It stays off and is not touched again by the mode logic.
On → Turn the device on when the mode activates. It stays on and is not touched again by the mode logic.
💡 Mode settings and overload protection are two separate things.
A mode fires once when activated — one action, done. The priority list (overload protection) can still turn a device off temporarily if power exceeds the limit — completely independent of the mode.

Example: If Off is selected for a water heater in Night mode, it turns off at night start and stays off all night. If — is used instead, the water heater continues normally — but Power Guard can still shed it mid-night if power exceeds the limit.

Set a Night schedule to automatically switch between Home and Night at chosen times. Power Guard never restores a device that is currently being throttled — safety always takes priority.

    Charging Schedule

    ⚡ How to set up automatic scheduling ▼
    1. In the Devices tab — find your charger and fill in Battery size (kWh) and Charge to (%).
    2. In Homey Flows — create a flow:
      WHEN your car app reports battery % changed
      THEN Power Guard → "Report [battery %] battery for [charger]"
      Drag the battery % tag from your car app (Audi, Nissan, Tesla, etc.) into the value field.
    3. Set a Finish charging by time below and click Save schedule.
    How the smart charging logic works:
    Power Guard picks the N cheapest hours before your deadline and charges at maximum only during those hours. All other hours the charger is off.
    • Cheapest hours selected — charger runs at maximum to fill the battery as fast as possible during the cheapest electricity.
    • All other hours — charger is off, regardless of price level.
    • When fewer than (hours needed + 1h buffer) remain before the deadline — charging switches to maximum regardless of price to guarantee the car is ready.
    How many hours are needed to charge the car. Auto-calculated when a car device is linked or battery % is reported via Flow. Set a fixed number here if neither is configured.
    h
    e.g. 07:00. Power Guard picks the N cheapest hours before this time and charges at max only during those. Leave empty to use price-only mode.
